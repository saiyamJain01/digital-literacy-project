# 🛡️ Digital Ambassador: Stay Safe Online Checklist
> **Project:** Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention  
> **Role:** Digital Ambassador (Peer Support)

This repository contains a curated checklist to help college students in India navigate the digital world securely. Stay alert, stay safe!

---

## 📋 Prevention Checklist

Use this checklist to audit your digital hygiene. You can even check these off if you're viewing this on GitHub!

- **1. The Golden UPI Rule:** Never enter your **UPI PIN to receive money**. If someone claims to "send" you a refund or prize and asks for your PIN, it’s a 100% scam.
- **2. Verify UPI Identity:** Before clicking 'Pay' or scanning a QR, always double-check the **Verified Name** on the app (GPay/PhonePe). Scammers use fake business aliases.
- **3. Enable MFA (2FA):** Turn on **Multi-Factor Authentication** for your VIT student mail, WhatsApp, and Instagram. A password alone is no longer enough protection.
- **4. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Never check bank balances or do UPI transactions on free campus/cafe Wi-Fi. Always switch to **Mobile Data** for financial tasks.
- **5. Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check which apps have access to your **SMS, Contacts, and Microphone**. Delete any "utility" apps (cleaners, flashlights) that ask for too much.
- **6. Spot the 'Digital Arrest' Scam:** Remember, no police or government agency will ever "arrest" you over a WhatsApp video call or demand money to settle a case. **Just Hang Up.**
- **7. Think Before You Click:** Avoid clicking shortened links (bit.ly/tinyurl) regarding "KYC Updates" or "Account Blocked." Always use the official bank/service app.
- **8. Scrub Your Socials:** Limit personal info like your home address, birth year, or real-time location on public profiles. This data is gold for hackers crafting phishing attacks.

## 🚨 Emergency Reporting Channels

If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime, **speed is key.**

| Channel | Contact Information |
| :--- | :--- |
| **National Helpline** | 📞 **1930** (Available 24/7) |
| **Official Portal** | 🌐 [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in) |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> For financial fraud, report within the **"Golden Hour"** (first 60 minutes) to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds.

## 📝 Project Report: Reflection

**What surprised me most:** During my research, I was genuinely shocked by the psychological depth of **"Digital Arrest" scams**. It’s not just a technical hack; it’s a mental one. The way scammers use fake police setups and legal jargon to keep well-educated students on a video call for hours while draining their accounts is terrifying. It made me realize that cybercrime is often more about exploiting **human fear** than exploiting software bugs.

**The habit I will change:** As a direct result of this project, I am changing how I handle **App Permissions**. I realized I had several third-party apps with access to my SMS and contacts without a clear reason. I have now conducted a "permission purge" and will strictly avoid sideloading any APKs from outside the official Play Store. This "zero-trust" approach to my phone's data is my new baseline.
